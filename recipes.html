Casados are  “typical” Costa Rican Plates consisting of a meat accompanied usually by rice, black beans, and plantains. This appetizer plate uses Tostones as the star of the dish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 unripe plantains
  • 1/2 cup of oil
  • Salt
  • Black beans
  • Sour cream
  • Cream cheese
  • Salsa

Instructions:

  1. Peel the plantains and cut into 1 inch thick slices.
  2. In a deep frying pan heat the oil and fry the plantains till golden.
  3. Flatten the plantains using a tortilla press to about a third of an inch thick.
  4. Fry the plantains again until golden yellow on both sides.
  5. Let cool on paper towel.
  6. Serve with cream cheese, black beans, sour cream, and salsa.

 

This variation on a classic German/Austrian Schnitzel recipe calls for veal cutlets that are served with a rich creamy mushroom gravy. 

Ingredients:

  • 6 slices of veal (pork loin may be substituted), pounded thin between sheets of wax paper.
  • 1 egg - Beaten
  • Canola oil for frying

Dredge:

  • 2 cups Bread crumbs
  • 1 tsp. Onion Powder
  • 1 tsp. Garlic Powder
  • 1 tsp. dill
  • 1 tsp. Salt
  • ½ tsp. Pepper
  • 1 tsp. Nutmeg
  • 1 tsp. Sweet Paprika
  • 2 tsp dried parsley

Sauce:

  • 1 Large Onion, chopped
  • 1 pkg sliced mushrooms
  • ½ tsp. salt
  • ½ tsp. paprika
  • ½ tsp. garlic powder
  • ½ tsp. onion powder
  • ½ tsp. nutmeg
  • 1 pinch of pepper
  • 16 oz. Sour Cream
  • 1 tbsp. Flour

Instructions:

Meat:

  1. Prepare dredge by placing the all but the parsley in a food processor.
  2.  Pulse until thoroughly mixed.
  3. Blend in parsley by hand before use.
  4. Coat pieces of meat in egg and dredge.
  5. Sauté untill golden on both sides.
  6. Place in warm oven to hold.

Sauce:

  1. Add more oil to skillet.
  2. Sauté onions and mushrooms.
  3. Sprinkle and toss in dry seasonings.
  4. Beat flour into sour cream.
  5. Turn off heat and stir into skillet when ready to serve.
  6. Serve over meat with veggies of choice.

Chili Crab is Singapore's National dish! Try this easy at home recipe!!

Ingredients:

  • 6 Blue Swimmer crabs
  • 6 green onions
  • 3 Tbsp. peanut oil
  • 1 Tbsp. Fresh cracked peppercorn
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 3 small red chili’s – chopped
  • 2 tsp. grated ginger
  • ½ cup tomato sauce
  • ½ cup sweet chili sauce (Mae Ploy)
  • 1 cup fish stock
  • 1/3 cup Hoisin sauce
  • 2 Tbsp. fish sauce
  • 1 Tbsp. sugar
  • 1 tsp. salt

Instructions:

  • Prepare crabs by cleaning and cutting into segments.
  • Crack crab claws
  • Slice Green onion diagonally, separating white and green sections.
  • Heat oil in large fry pan or wok.
  • Add garlic, chili, black pepper, and ginger and cool over medium heat until fragrant.
  • Add tomato sauce, chili sauce, fish stock, Hoisin sauce, fish sauce, sugar, and white part of the onion.
  • Stir to combine the ingredients, bring to a boil.
  • Add crabs and allow to simmer with lid on for approximately 10-15 minutes, until all shells have turned orange and meat is opaque.
  • Taste and add salt if needed.
  • Serve garnished with the remaining green onions on top and accompanied by steamed jasmine rice.
